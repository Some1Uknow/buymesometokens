
# Buy Me Some Tokens — Brand & Logo Spec

## Concept

**"Buy Me a Coffee" for AI agents.** The coffee shop ritual transposed into crypto — instead of buying a friend a latte, you send OG tokens directly to an agent's wallet. The brand lives at the intersection of:

- **Coffee culture** (warmth, ritual, gratitude, daily habit)
- **Crypto** (on-chain, non-custodial, verifiable)
- **AI agents** (autonomous, machine intelligence, digital workers)

The tone is warm but sharp. Welcoming but unapologetically technical.

## Wordmark Lockup

The wordmark should **never** accompany the icon in a traditional logo lockup — NeoBrutalism separates them. The wordmark is always set in Space Grotesk (or Inter if Grotesk unavailable):

- **"BUY ME SOME"** — weight 700, letter-spacing -0.02em
- **"TOKENS"** — weight 900, letter-spacing -0.03em, set in yellow `#FFE600` with a black offset shadow (3px right, 3px down)

Full lockup available formats:
- **Horizontal** (desktop): Icon left, wordmark right
- **Stacked** (mobile): Icon centered above, wordmark below

## Brand Visuals

### Photography / Illustration Style
- High-contrast flat vector illustrations
- Bold, graphic, poster-like compositions
- No photos, no gradients, no soft lighting
- Subjects: coffee cups, robots, circuit patterns, blockchain blocks, hands tipping coins

### Texture
- Optional grain texture overlay at 8-12% opacity on the beige background
- Never on dark sections

### Pattern / Background Elements
- Large overlapping geometric blocks (squares, rectangles) in pastel colors
- Offset grid patterns in thin black lines

## Brand Voice Guidelines (for non-logo use)

- Always communicate in short, declarative sentences
- Use UPPERCASE for headings and calls to action
- Avoid emojis in copy (icons and marks do the work)
- Use arrows (→ ↗ ↓ ←) instead of chevrons or bullets
- Address the reader as "you" — direct and personal
- Technical terms are welcome but always explained in 4 words or fewer
